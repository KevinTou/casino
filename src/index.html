Blackjack ("21" or "twenty-one")

About How to Play Terminology Rules Basic Strategy

About

Blackjack is one of the most common games found inside of a casino. It is a card game where the player plays against the dealer.

How to Play

The object of the game is to have your cards total higher than the dealer's hand, without "busting" or going over a total of 21. The dealer begins the game by dealing each player two cards and the dealer one card face-up and one card face-down.

Terminology

Blackjack:
a two-card twenty-one (21), usually with an Ace and any ten-valued (10) card. This is usually paid with three-to-two odds (3:2) as opposed to even money (1:1)
Bet:
a wager or the amount that the player wishes to gamble
Face cards:
cards with a value of ten (10). Face cards are usually denoted as Jack, Queen, or King. 10s can also be considered a "face card"
Insurance:
this occurs when the dealer's face up card is an Ace. The player has the option to wager up to half of their bet, if they think the dealer has a Blackjack, to "save" their bet. If the player makes the insurance bet and the dealer does not have Blackjack, then the player loses their insurance bet and the game continues as normal
Even money:
when a bet is paid the same amount (1:1)
Surrender:
while the player still has their initial two cards, they can decide to surrender if they think they cannot beat the dealer. The player gives up half of their bet to the dealer and keeps the other half
Double-Down:
the act of placing an additional bet (up to the original bet) and receiving ONLY one card. This can only occur when the player's hand has two cards
Split:
if you are dealt two cards of equal value, you have the option of "splitting" them into two separate hands. You must match your original bet. Y
Soft hand:
a hand where the Ace can be considered 1 or 11
Hard hand:
a hand where the Ace is considered 11
Bust/Busting:
a bust, or busting hand, is a losing hand where the total value goes over 21 and therefore, an automatic loss
Hit:
receive an additional card
Stay/Stand:
taking no more cards
Push:
having the same value as the dealer, which results in neither a win nor a loss
*Draw:
this has two meanings in Blackjack. Draw can either refer to "drawing a card/taking a hit" or a "draw/tie/push"

Rules

  1. The game of Blackjack starts with either a single deck, double deck, six deck, or eight deck of cards. There are other variants but these are usually the most common.
  2. The game usually starts with the dealer burning a card, then the players are dealt their cards (starting with the player on the dealer's left).
  3. The game begins with each player having two cards in their hand and the dealer having two cards as well (but only one card being "exposed").
  4. If the dealer's exposed card is a ten-value card, the dealer will check to see if they have Blackjack, in which case, the hand will end. If the dealer's exposed card is an Ace, the dealer will offer insurance. If the player has Blackjack, they can taken even money, or gamble that the dealer doesn't have Blackjack. If the dealer has Blackjack, then all the non-Blackjack hands will lose unless the player has a Blackjack themselves (in which case, the hand will push) and the insurance bets, if any are made, will be paid.
  5. If the dealer does not have a Blackjack, then the hand will procede as normal and the player will decide what to do based on their hand.

    • Stay

  10. Based on the house rules, the dealer will continue to hit (if at all) until they reach a total of 17 (usually a hard 17).

Basic Strategy

When playing Blackjack, the purpose of the game is to beat the dealer. A common misconception that people have when playing Blackjack, is that they need to get as close as possible to 21. Although having a good hand helps, it's not really necessary to win.
