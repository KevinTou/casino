Terminology

Blackjack:
a two-card twenty-one (21), usually with an Ace and any ten-valued (10) card. This is usually paid with three-to-two odds (3:2) as opposed to even money (1:1)
Bet:
a wager or the amount that the player wishes to gamble
Face cards:
cards with a value of ten (10). Face cards are usually denoted as Jack, Queen, or King. 10s can also be considered a "face card"
Insurance:
this occurs when the dealer's face up card is an Ace. The player has the option to wager up to half of their bet, if they think the dealer has a Blackjack, to "save" their bet. If the player makes the insurance bet and the dealer does not have Blackjack, then the player loses their insurance bet and the game continues as normal
Even money:
when a bet is paid the same amount (1:1)
Surrender:
while the player still has their initial two cards, they can decide to surrender if they think they cannot beat the dealer. The player gives up half of their bet to the dealer and keeps the other half
Double-Down:
the act of placing an additional bet (up to the original bet) and receiving ONLY one card. This can only occur when the player's hand has two cards
Split:
if you are dealt two cards of equal value, you have the option of "splitting" them into two separate hands. You must match your original bet. Y
Soft hand:
a hand where the Ace can be considered 1 or 11
Hard hand:
a hand where the Ace is considered 11
Bust/Busting:
a bust, or busting hand, is a losing hand where the total value goes over 21 and therefore, an automatic loss
Hit:
receive an additional card
Stay/Stand:
taking no more cards
Push:
having the same value as the dealer, which results in neither a win nor a loss
*Draw:
this has two meanings in Blackjack. Draw can either refer to "drawing a card/taking a hit" or a "draw/tie/push"
